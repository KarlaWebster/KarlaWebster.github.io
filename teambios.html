Abigail Goss

Abbie attended Franklin College and graduated in 2013 with a B.A. in psychology and a minor in sociology. She found her passion working with children who have disabilities when she was in high school and since then has worked with children with autism. Abbie was not sure what career path she wanted to take with children until she heard of ABA, and she immediately knew that this was what she wanted to do. It is her passion in life to help change the lives of children with disabilities and to watch these children grow into the best individuals they can be. In her free time, Abbie likes to go to the lake, camp, boat, play soccer, and hang out with friends, family, and her puppy Delilah.

 

Alexandra Parshall

Alexandra grew up in Carmel, and graduated from Cathedral High School in 2011. In the Spring of 2015, she graduated from Indiana University Bloomington with a B.A. in Speech Pathology and Audiology, and a minor in Spanish. Alexandra had always loved working with children, but knew it was her calling after she became involved with Indiana University's Dance Marathon. IUDM is an annual weekend dance-a-thon where college students stand and dance for 36 straight hours to raise money for the children of Riley Children's Hospital, all while playing and interacting with the kids. Alexandra loves helping kids to grown and overcome their obstacles. In her free time, Alexandra loves to travel. She backpacked around Europe for 70 days after graduation. She also loves to read, be outdoors, practice yoga, and spend time with her family, friends, and multiple pets.

 

Alexandra Zaiger

Alex graduated from Ball State University in 2014 with a degree in both elementary and special education. She then went on to teach Pre-Kindergarten in Carmel for one year. Born and raised in Carmel, her hobbies include running and biking on the monon, trying new restaurants, and spending time with her friends and family. She is passionate about working with children and helping them succeed which has led her to a career in ABA.

 

Allison Moore

Allison graduated from Indiana University Bloomington in 2010 with a BA in Speech and Hearing, and a minor in American Studies. Throughout high school, she always had babysitting jobs and worked with one child with autism, which sparked her interest. She also played basketball and volleyball, which led to coaching experiences with young girls. Prior to joining the Bierman team, she worked in the field of ABA for 3 ½ years. She is currently enrolled in the Ball State Master’s program acquiring her Master’s Degree in Special Education, with a focus on autism. She has always loved working with children, which is why spending time with her nephew and niece is one of her favorite past times. She also enjoys spending time with her family, traveling, rescuing animals, attending sporting events and concerts, her dog, Andy, and spending time with her friends doing outdoor activities (especially boating).

 

Allison Smith

Allie graduated from Ball State University in 2012 with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Elementary Education with a Mathematics Minor. She has worked with children with Autism for 5 years, and has worked directly with children for over 9 years. Allie comes to Bierman with 2 years of ABA experience in other clinical settings. She enjoys fostering animals in need of homes, and has two rescues of her own.

 

Amanda Connelly

Amanda has a wide variety of experience with people with disabilities, from camps to respite care to group homes and other residential settings. Before joining Bierman ABA, Amanda was an ABA therapist at an accredited ABA school in Atlanta for over four years. While there, she led a classroom of elementary and middle school children with Autism and other developmental disabilities. She has a B.A. in Psychology from Georgia State University and is currently pursuing her Masters in ABA through Ball State.

About Bierman ABA

Bierman ABA was established in 2006 with a simple focus on providing great therapy for children with autism and on building a unique, awesome and friendly work culture. Since then, it has been truly gratifying to have helped so many children and to have watched their progress with things such as signs, speech and behavioral development. As we’ve grown, we’ve kept a laser sharp focus on our mission and founding principles.
